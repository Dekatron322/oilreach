SamyungTV AIX3-series

SamyungTV AIX3-series ensures that reliable, easy access to satellite TV in digital picture and sound on yacht while sailing or mooring. AIX3-series consist of a stabilized 39cm (15″)~120cm(48″) diameter antenna enclosed in an attractive white radome and an ACU(Antenna Control Unit).

The ACU has the operation interface for the communication with Samyung TV antenna via RS232C port. Aix3-390 and Aix3-450 is designed for power and small yacht. Aix3-390 and Aix3-450 is smallest size and lightest weight.

Built-in GPS and 3-axis servo stabilization tracking system will be serving you fully stabilized TV channel from the satellite. Aix3-610 and Aix3-850 provides increased signal reception area at sea and stronger signal reception in inclement weather because the larger dish is able to acquire more of the satellite’s signal and increase the antenna gain.

Search and Resuce Radar Transpoder

The 9 GHz Search & Rescue Radar Transponder (SART) is a self-contained, waterproof radar transponder intended for emergency use at sea. The Radar-SART is used to locate a survival craft or distressed vessel by creating a series of dots on a rescuing ship’s radar display.

A SART will only respond to a 9 GHz X-band (3 cm wavelength) radar. It will not be seen on S-band (10 cm) or other radar. Shipboard Global Maritime Distress Safety System (GMDSS) include one or more search and rescue locating devices.

The Autopilot

The autopilot on board of ships is designed to maintain as close as possible azimuth to the desired course. Sounds simple enough but is not.

As a result a ship trajectory is an oscillation around the desired direction. The autopilot has to amortize this oscillation as much as possible or desired.

ECDIS State of the Art Navigation

ECDIS (SE-3000) State-of-the-art navigation supporting system displaying the chart information identical with that in the paper charts in a selective manner and providing real-time integrated navigation information to set up the optimum route and ultimately to serve to prevent probable maritime accidents e.g. collision, grounding, etc.

RADAR Display, ARPA overlay. AIS information display (display identified and unidentified symbols). Integrated display and monitoring of (D)GPS, Gyro Compass, RADAR, Speed, NAVTEX, Weather information. Planning of waypoints and alternate route.

Specifying prohibited area and safety contour. Constant and real-time monitoring of ship’s positioning. Target plotting and monitoring. Real-time automatic route recording. Automatic alarms and indications of off-track from the planned route or other dangers.

Felcom 19 System

The FELCOM 19 is FURUNO’s new Inmarsat-C Mobile Earth Station. It provides a high quality two-way telex and data link between ships and other parties at sea or on land. All functions and services of the Inmarsat-C system are provided: EGC (Safety NET/FleetNET), distress message handling, two-way digital store-and-forward messaging including polling, data reporting, E-mail, etc. Distress alert are initiated by the remote distress alert unit.

The distress message including own ship’s position is easily edited. A PC can be used for editing the message via Ethernet when it is supplied with dedicated software. The FELCOM 19 consists of an antenna and display unit with keyboard.

The display unit embodies a 10.4″ color LCD and processor unit. The distress alert button is incorporated in the display for emergency. Data files and utility program on floppy disks are easily loaded into the processor through the front loading disk drive in the display unit.

The compact design of display unit and omni-directional antenna unit offer space-saving installation. An optional GPS module is available for position reporting without operator’s intervention. GMDSS compliance can be achieved by adding a printer and a AC/DC power supply unit.

VHF Radio

VHF radios (Very High Frequency) operate between 136-174 MHz. The advantage to a VHF walkie-talkie is that it can cover more distance with less power because VHF waves are longer and remain closer to the ground. … Another benefit of VHF waves is that they will penetrate trees and foliage better than UHF radios.

We program vessel’s information in all major brands of Radios and AIS. Radio Programming is required when the Flag of a vessel is changed and the vessel is issued with new MMSI Number. The radio programming cannot be carried out by users as it is protected as required by regulations and an experienced and qualified radio technician is required to do this for the owners of the vessel.

Oilreach Limited is equipped with all necessary information and experienced radio technicians who can perform the job successfully. In case of change of vessel’s MMSI Number, following radio equipment is required to be programmed; VHF DSC(s). MF/HF DSC(s). AIS. SSAS, Some equipment are programmed onboard the vessel and some are updated by the service provider. EPIRB(s).
